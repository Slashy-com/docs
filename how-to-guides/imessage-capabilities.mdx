The iMessage agent is your personal email assistant via text. Everything the AI can do in the Slashy sidebar, it can do through iMessage. Text instructions in plain English.

<Tip>Think of iMessage as a remote control for your inbox. You can do everything from your phone that you would do on your laptop -- without opening any app.</Tip>

## Search Emails

Find any email with natural language. The agent searches your entire inbox history.

- *"Find the last email from Sarah about the Q4 budget"*
- *"Show me unread emails from today"*
- *"Did John ever reply to my proposal?"*

## Visual Email Previews

The agent renders any email as a screenshot and sends it in the conversation -- full layout, formatting, and images.

- *"Show me the email from Alex about the partnership"*
- *"Send me that invoice from yesterday"*

## Draft and Send Replies

Tell the agent what to say. Review the draft via preview, then iterate before sending.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Request a draft">
    Text: *"Reply to Sarah's email saying I'll have the report ready by Thursday"*
  </Step>
  <Step title="Review the preview">
    The agent sends a visual preview of the draft for your review.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Iterate or send">
    Reply with *"send it"*, *"make it more formal"*, or *"add a line about the timeline"* to refine. The agent keeps adjusting until you approve.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Warning>Saying *"send it"* actually sends the email. If you want to save it as a draft to review on desktop, say *"save as draft"* instead.</Warning>

## Daily Briefings

Set up a [recurring briefing](/how-to-guides/imessage-briefings) or request on demand:

- *"Give me my morning briefing"*
- *"What's on my plate today?"*

Covers important unread emails, today's calendar, pending replies, and dropped balls.

## Meeting Prep

Get attendee research and context before any meeting.

- *"Prep me for my 2pm meeting"*
- *"Research the attendees of my call with Sequoia tomorrow"*

Pulls LinkedIn profiles, past email history, and relevant memories for each attendee.

<Frame><img src="/images/screenshots/imessage-meeting-prep.png" alt="iMessage meeting prep briefing with attendee backgrounds" /></Frame>

## Voice Messages

Send a voice message through iMessage. The agent transcribes it and acts on your instructions -- useful when you are driving, walking, or in between meetings.

<Tip>Voice messages are great for complex requests. Instead of typing *"Find all emails from investors this month where I haven't replied and draft updates for each,"* just say it out loud.</Tip>

## Set Reminders

- *"Remind me to follow up with Jane on Friday"*
- *"If Mike doesn't reply by Wednesday, remind me"*

## Manage Calendar

- *"Schedule a 30-minute meeting with sarah@acme.com on Tuesday at 10am"*
- *"Move my 3pm to 4pm"*
- *"Am I free Thursday afternoon?"*

Creates events with conferencing links and checks for conflicts automatically.

## Manage Automations

- *"List my active automations"*
- *"Turn off the receipt forwarding automation"*
- *"Add ceo@company.com to my VIP alert list"*

## Web Search

The agent searches the web via Perplexity AI on your behalf.

- *"What's the latest funding round for Acme Corp?"*

## Power User Tips

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Be specific with email references">
    *"Reply to Sarah's email about the budget"* works better than *"reply to that email."* The more context you give, the faster the agent finds the right thread.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Chain multiple actions">
    Complex multi-step tasks work in a single message: *"Find all emails from investors where I haven't replied, draft a short update for each, and list them for my review."*
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Use voice for long instructions">
    Send a voice message for anything longer than a sentence. The agent transcribes and executes -- no typing needed.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Save drafts for desktop review">
    If you want to polish a reply on your laptop before sending, say *"save as draft"* instead of *"send it."* The draft will be waiting in Slashy when you open it.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
