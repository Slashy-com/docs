title: "Why Using Slashy as Primary Makes AI Smarter"
description: "How read, send, and interaction signals compound to make drafts, labels, and the agent dramatically better over time."
---

Slashy works alongside Gmail without issues. But AI features -- draft quality, classification accuracy, autocomplete, and agent usefulness -- improve significantly faster when Slashy is where you do your email.

## How Your Actions Train the AI

| What You Do in Slashy | What the AI Learns |
|---|---|
| Send an email | Your writing style for that recipient and context |
| Accept a draft unchanged | The approach was correct -- reinforce it |
| Edit a draft, then send | The diff trains future drafts toward your edits |
| Dismiss a draft | The approach was wrong -- avoid similar drafts |
| Read without replying | This type doesn't need a response |
| Move email to different label | Classification correction for similar emails |

These signals are contextual. Editing an investor reply teaches investor tone -- it doesn't change how the AI drafts team updates.

## Why Dual-Wielding Gmail Limits Learning

Emails you send, read, or reclassify in Gmail are invisible to the learning system. The AI misses:

- **Drafting patterns** from Gmail-composed emails
- **Draft dismissals** when you bypass Slashy suggestions
- **Read patterns** showing what you prioritize
- **Label corrections** made in Gmail's interface

<Warning>Splitting email between both clients means the AI takes roughly twice as long to reach the same accuracy. Some behavioral learning (read/archive patterns) never fully catches up.</Warning>

## The Compounding Effect

- **Week 1:** Drafts are decent but generic. Labels catch obvious categories.
- **Week 2-3:** After 20-30 interactions, tone starts matching for common email types. Labels improve noticeably.
- **Month 2-3:** Drafts sound like you wrote them. The agent anticipates preferences.
- **Month 6+:** Drafts regularly go out with little editing. Classification rarely needs correction.

Users who commit from day one reach "rarely edit drafts" in 4-6 weeks. Dual-wielders take 3-4 months.

## What to Do Right Now

<Steps>
  <Step title="Install the desktop app">
    [Download here](/getting-started/desktop-app) so Slashy is one shortcut away.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Replace your Gmail bookmark">
    Set Slashy as your default startup page.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Switch notifications">
    Turn off Gmail notifications. Use Slashy's notifications instead.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Use AI Draft Reply on everything">
    Press `Cmd+Enter` on every email for the first week. Accept, edit, or dismiss -- each action trains the AI.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Correct label misclassifications">
    Each correction directly improves future accuracy.
  </Step>
</Steps>

The AI cannot learn from what it cannot see. The more of your email life happens inside Slashy, the faster it becomes the assistant you want.
