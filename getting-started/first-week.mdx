Slashy replaces your Gmail tab with an AI email client that learns how you write, drafts replies, and automates busywork. Here is what to expect from signup through day 7.

## The Onboarding Guide (15 Minutes)

A 7-step guide walks you through setup. Complete it in one sitting or come back later.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Auth">
    Sign in with Google to create your Slashy account.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Connect Gmail">
    Grant access via OAuth 2.0. See [Connecting Gmail](/getting-started/connecting-gmail) for exactly what you are granting.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Memories">
    Edit your role, company, and communication style. The AI also auto-generates memories from your email history.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Bulk Cleanup">
    Mass unsubscribe and block senders ranked by volume. Clean up years of clutter in one pass.
  </Step>
  <Step title="iMessage">
    Connect your phone number for alerts, summaries, and OTP forwarding via iMessage. Optional but recommended.
  </Step>
  <Step title="iOS App">
    Download the [Slashy mobile app](/getting-started/mobile).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Done">
    Slashy begins syncing your inbox. You are ready.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Day-by-Day Guide

<Steps>
  <Step title="Day 1: Sync, Set Up, Explore">
    **Automatic:** Gmail syncs (minutes to an hour), AI analyzes your sent emails, importance classification starts.

    **Do this:**
    - Read emails in Slashy. Use `j`/`k` to navigate, `Enter` to open, `e` to archive.
    - Try the command palette (`Cmd+K`).
    - Set theme and compose mode in Settings (`Cmd+,`).
    - Add extra Gmail accounts in **Settings > Account > Connected Accounts**.

    <Tip>Press `Shift+?` for the keyboard shortcuts reference. Shortcuts save 20+ minutes per day.</Tip>
  </Step>
  <Step title="Day 2: Start Using AI Drafts">
    The AI now has enough sent-email data to generate useful drafts.

    - Press `Cmd+Enter` to generate an AI draft. Edit before sending -- your edits train the AI.
    - Use autocomplete while composing: press `Tab` to accept ghost text suggestions.
    - Accept or dismiss auto-suggested drafts. Both actions train the AI.

    <Tip>Draft 3-5 replies with `Cmd+Enter` and edit each one. This gives the AI a strong style baseline.</Tip>
  </Step>
  <Step title="Day 3: First Automation">
    Open the AI agent sidebar (`Cmd+Shift+L`) and set up the **Daily Dropped Ball Scanner**: a recurring scan for unanswered emails, delivered via iMessage each morning.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Day 4: Master the Calendar">
    - Press `2` for Calendar view, `1` for email.
    - Create events by clicking and dragging time slots.
    - In replies, press `Shift+Cmd+A` to insert available time slots.
    - Connect Zoom in **Settings > Calendars** for auto-generated meeting links.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Day 5: Clean Up and Organize">
    - Create snippets (`n s`) for frequent reply types. Insert with `Cmd+Shift+S`.
    - Add contact memories via the contact sidebar (`p i`).
    - Review your AI memories in Settings and add any the AI missed about your communication style.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Day 6: Add More Automations">
    Good picks for your second batch:

    | Automation | Best For |
    |---|---|
    | Meeting Prep Briefing | 3+ external meetings/week |
    | Receipt Forwarding | Corporate card holders |
    | VIP Email Alerts | Can't-miss contacts |
    | OTP Forwarding | Email-based 2FA codes |
  </Step>
  <Step title="Day 7: Review and Tune">
    - Check AI draft quality. Add specific memories if drafts miss the mark (e.g., "I sign off with 'Best,' not 'Thanks'").
    - Review automations in **Settings > Automations**. Toggle off anything unused.
    - Ask the agent: *"What are my dropped balls right now?"* for an on-demand scan.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## What to Expect After Week 1

| Day 1 | Day 7 |
|---|---|
| AI drafts need heavy editing | Drafts need light edits or none |
| Importance sorting occasionally miscategorizes | Sorting accurate for most senders |
| You check email manually | Automations surface what matters first |
| You type every reply | Autocomplete handles routine sentences |
| You forget follow-ups | Dropped ball scanner catches them daily |

The AI keeps improving. Most users report drafts rarely need editing by week 3.
