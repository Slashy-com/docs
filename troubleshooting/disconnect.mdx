Whether you need to temporarily pause the AI, disconnect an inbox, or delete your account, here are your options and what happens to your data at each step.

## Pause the AI Agent (Read Mode)

Toggle **Read Mode** in Settings > Account. The agent can still search, read, and draft for your review, but cannot send emails, archive, modify labels, or create events. Action-taking automations pause; read-only automations (like dropped ball scanner) continue.

Toggle Read Mode off to resume full functionality.

## Disconnect an Inbox

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open Settings">
    Settings > Account > Connected Accounts.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Disconnect">
    Click **Disconnect** next to the inbox.
  </Step>
</Steps>

**What happens:** Sync stops, automations disable, tracking stops, agent loses access. Your emails in Gmail are completely unaffected. Your Slashy account still exists and you can reconnect anytime.

## Reconnect After Disconnect

Go to Settings > Account, click **Add Account**, and sign in with the same Google account. Slashy re-syncs your history (2-15 minutes). Previous labels, memories, and automations may still be available.

## Revoke Access via Google

For maximum certainty, revoke from Google's side:

1. Go to [myaccount.google.com/permissions](https://myaccount.google.com/permissions)
2. Find **Slashy** and click **Remove Access**
3. This immediately revokes the OAuth token

Re-authorization through onboarding is required to reconnect.

## What Happens to Your Data

| Action | Data Impact |
|---|---|
| **Read Mode** | All data preserved. Agent stops taking actions. |
| **Disconnect inbox** | Slashy account preserved. Cached data remains but stops updating. |
| **Revoke via Google** | Immediate access loss. Slashy account exists but inbox is disconnected. |
| **Delete account** | All Slashy data permanently deleted. Gmail unaffected. Irreversible. |

## Delete Your Account

Email [founders@slashy.com](mailto:founders@slashy.com) from your Slashy email address and request deletion. All data is removed: account, memories, automations, snippets, cached email data.

<Warning>Account deletion is permanent and cannot be undone. If you might come back, consider disconnecting instead.</Warning>
