Shortwave proved that AI belongs in email. Slashy takes that further -- instead of summarizing your inbox, the AI agent drafts replies, runs automations, and takes action on your behalf.

## What Feels Familiar

- **AI-powered inbox** -- machine learning sorts and prioritizes email
- **Smart organization** -- automatic grouping and labeling
- **Bundles / Labels** -- emails sorted into categories
- **Modern UI** -- clean, fast, keyboard-friendly interface

## The Key Difference

**Shortwave AI summarizes. Slashy AI acts.**

| Shortwave | Slashy |
|---|---|
| AI summarizes threads | AI drafts full replies in your voice |
| AI answers questions about your inbox | AI answers questions *and* takes action (send, archive, label, schedule) |
| Bundles group similar emails | Importance labels auto-sort with AI that learns from your corrections |
| No automations | Background automations: dropped ball scanner, meeting prep, VIP alerts |
| No iMessage | iMessage integration for alerts, summaries, and OTP forwarding |
| No calendar | Full calendar with booking links, availability insert, Meet With overlay |
| Web app only | Desktop app, web app, and iOS app |

## Shortwave Bundles vs Slashy Labels

Shortwave bundles group emails by type. Slashy importance labels do the same but are AI-classified and trainable:

- Move an email to a different label and the AI learns from the correction
- Customize label names and descriptions to match your workflow
- Labels are bidirectional with Gmail -- changes sync both ways

## Setting Up Snippets

Slashy Snippets replace any templates you used in Shortwave or Gmail canned responses.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Create a snippet">
    Press `n s` to open the snippet creator. Add a name, shortcut key, and body text.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Insert while composing">
    Press `Cmd+Shift+S` to search and insert any snippet.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Use variables">
    Add `{{first_name}}`, `{{company}}`, or custom fields. Slashy auto-fills from contact data.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Full Feature Comparison

| Capability | Shortwave | Slashy |
|---|---|---|
| **AI inbox sorting** | Bundles | AI importance labels (trainable) |
| **AI thread summaries** | Yes | Yes |
| **AI drafts** | Limited | Full drafts in your voice + autocomplete |
| **AI agent** | Q&A only | Conversational agent that takes action |
| **Automations** | None | Email triggers, calendar triggers, reminders |
| **Calendar** | None | Full view + booking links + availability insert |
| **iMessage integration** | None | Alerts, summaries, OTP forwarding |
| **Desktop app** | Web only | Native macOS app + web |
| **Mobile app** | Android + iOS | iOS (Android coming soon) |
| **Snippets / templates** | None | AI-powered snippets with variable substitution |
| **Read receipts** | None | Built-in email tracking |
| **Search** | AI-powered | AI-powered natural language |

## Migration Checklist

<Steps>
  <Step title="Connect your Gmail account">
    Sign up at [slashy.com](https://slashy.com) and connect via Google OAuth. Full email history syncs in 2-15 minutes.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Replace Shortwave Bundles with labels">
    Go to **Settings > Labels** and configure importance labels. These are Slashy's version of Bundles, but they learn from your corrections and sync with Gmail.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add AI memories">
    **Settings > Memories** -- describe your role, writing style, and preferences so AI drafts match your voice from day one.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Try AI Draft Reply">
    Open any thread and press `Cmd+Enter` to generate a full reply draft. Edit and send.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Set up your first automation">
    Open the agent sidebar (`Cmd+Shift+L`) and say "Set up a daily dropped ball scanner." This scans for emails that need follow-up -- something Shortwave cannot do.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Create snippets">
    Press `n s` to create reusable email templates with AI variable substitution (`{{first_name}}`, `{{company}}`).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Explore the calendar">
    Press `2` to open the full calendar view. Set up booking links in **Settings > Calendars** to replace any third-party scheduling tool.
  </Step>
</Steps>
